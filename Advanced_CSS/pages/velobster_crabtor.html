Description

A velobster crabtor is a bizarre, hybrid creature with a unique combination of features. Its head, with its sharp, serrated teeth and piercing eyes, resembles that of a fierce velociraptor. Its body, however, is more crustacean-like, with a hard, segmented shell and powerful claws. The tail, long and segmented, is reminiscent of a lobster's. This unusual combination of features suggests a creature that is both predatory and defensive, capable of both hunting and protecting itself from threats.

Species

Velobster crabtors have evolved into a diverse range of species, each with unique adaptations tailored to its specific environment. Some variants have developed more aquatic-focused traits, such as larger, more paddle-like claws for swimming and a more streamlined body shape. Others have become more terrestrial, with stronger legs for chasing prey, as well as a thicker, more protective shell. Their diet also varies widely, with some species specializing in fish and crustaceans, while others prey on larger vertebrates. This versatility has allowed velobster crabtors to thrive in a wide range of habitats, from coastal marshes to dense rainforests.
